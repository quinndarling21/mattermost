# Voice and tone

Voice is constant; tone shifts with context. This page defines both, plus the formatting rules that apply everywhere. Pair it with [brand.md](brand.md) and [lexicon.md](lexicon.md).

## Voice (always true)

Mattermost sounds like a senior engineer who respects your time: **clear, precise, technical, and calm.** We inform; we don't sell with pressure.

Our voice is:

- **Direct** - lead with the point, then support it.
- **Precise** - concrete capabilities over adjectives; correct technical terms.
- **Honest** - state requirements, limits, and trade-offs.
- **Respectful** - of the reader's intelligence and of the open-source community.
- **Confident, not loud** - no hype, no hard sell.

Our voice is **not** breathless, salesy, fearful, or padded with filler.

## Tone by context

Same voice, adjusted for the moment.

| Context | Tone | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Marketing / website | Confident, benefit-led, still concrete | Show the real capability and who it helps. No hype words. |
| Product docs / admin guides | Neutral, instructional, exact | Sentence-case headings, task-oriented steps, name settings and roles directly. |
| In-product microcopy | Brief, helpful, human | Short. Say what happened and what to do next. |
| Security bulletins / advisories | Factual, sober, zero hype | State the issue, severity, affected versions, and the fix. Never sensationalize. No marketing language. |
| Release notes / changelog | Plain, specific | What changed, why it matters, any action required. |
| Community / open source | Warm, collaborative, credit-giving | Acknowledge contributors; link to source and issues. |

## Formatting and mechanics

- **Product name:** Always **Mattermost**, one word, capital M. Never "MatterMost," "Matter Most," or "mattermost" in prose.
- **Capitalization:** Sentence case for headings and buttons ("Create channel," not "Create Channel"). Capitalize product feature names as defined in [lexicon.md](lexicon.md).
- **Voice grammar:** Active voice. Second person ("you") for the reader. Present tense where possible.
- **Sentences:** Short. One idea each. Cut words that don't add meaning.
- **Numbers and claims:** Every metric, percentage, certification, or comparison needs a real source. If there isn't one, don't make the claim.
- **Code and config:** Use inline code for settings, commands, endpoints, file paths, plugin IDs, and role IDs.
- **Punctuation:** At most one exclamation point per page, and usually zero. No ALL CAPS for emphasis.
- **Emoji:** Sparing to none in marketing and never in security content.
- **Accessibility:** Plain language, expand an acronym on first use, write descriptive link text (not "click here").

## Words and patterns to avoid

Full list with substitutes is in [lexicon.md](lexicon.md). The high-frequency offenders:

- Hype: *revolutionary, game-changing, next-generation, cutting-edge, world-class, best-in-class.*
- Filler: *seamless, frictionless, synergy, leverage (as a verb), supercharge, unlock, robust, powerful, simply, just.*
- Fear: *hackers are coming, unhackable, 100% secure, breach-proof.*
- Pressure: *act now, limited time, don't miss out.*

## A quick rewrite

- Off-brand: "Mattermost is a revolutionary, next-gen platform that seamlessly supercharges your team's collaboration and unlocks unstoppable productivity!"
- On-brand: "Mattermost brings messaging, workflows, and integrations into one self-hosted platform, so technical teams can collaborate on sensitive work without sending data to a third party."

The second version makes a real claim, names the audience, and a skeptical engineer can evaluate it.
