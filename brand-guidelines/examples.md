# Examples

Before/after rewrites that show the guidelines in action. Each "before" fails one or more rules in [brand.md](brand.md), [voice-and-tone.md](voice-and-tone.md), or [lexicon.md](lexicon.md); each "after" fixes it and notes why.

## 1. Hero headline

**Before:** "The world's most powerful, revolutionary collaboration platform that supercharges your team!"

**After:** "Secure, self-hosted collaboration for technical teams."

*Why:* Removed stacked superlatives ("most powerful," "revolutionary," "supercharges") and made a concrete, evaluable claim about what it is and who it's for.

## 2. Security section

**Before:** "Hackers are everywhere. Don't be the next data breach. Mattermost is 100% secure and completely unhackable."

**After:** "Run Mattermost on infrastructure you control, with granular access control and support for air-gapped deployments, so sensitive data never leaves your environment."

*Why:* Cut fear-based framing and the absolute claims ("100% secure," "unhackable"). Replaced them with the actual security mechanisms.

## 3. Feature description

**Before:** "Our cutting-edge AI seamlessly leverages next-gen synergy to unlock frictionless productivity."

**After:** "Playbooks turn repeatable work like incident response into checklists that run inside your channels, so the team follows the same steps every time."

*Why:* Removed undefined jargon ("synergy," "frictionless," "next-gen") and named a real feature, the workflow, and the benefit.

## 4. Integration claim

**Before:** "Mattermost seamlessly integrates with everything, effortlessly connecting all your tools."

**After:** "Mattermost connects to tools like GitLab, Jira, and PagerDuty through its plugin framework and webhooks. See the integrations directory for the current list."

*Why:* Replaced vague absolutes ("everything," "seamlessly," "effortlessly") with named integrations and a pointer to the source of truth.

## 5. Stat-driven proof point

**Before:** "Teams are 300% more productive and resolve incidents 10x faster with Mattermost."

**After:** "Mattermost centralizes incident response in one place: alerts, the response playbook, and the team discussion. (If you cite a metric, link the case study or benchmark it comes from.)"

*Why:* Unsourced numbers are forbidden. Either cite the source or describe the capability instead.

## 6. Call to action

**Before:** "Act now! Limited-time offer - don't miss your chance to transform your team forever!"

**After:** "Try the self-hosted edition, or talk to us about Cloud and enterprise options."

*Why:* Removed false urgency and "transform forever" hype. Gave the reader a real, low-pressure next step.

## 7. In-product microcopy

**Before:** "Oops! Something went wrong! :( Please try again later!!!"

**After:** "We couldn't send your message. Check your connection and try again."

*Why:* Said what happened and what to do next, in a calm tone, without excess punctuation.

## 8. Release note

**Before:** "We're thrilled to announce a massive, game-changing update packed with amazing improvements!"

**After:** "This release adds SAML group sync, fixes a channel-export bug, and updates the mobile push library. Admins using LDAP should review the upgrade notes before updating."

*Why:* Replaced hype with the specific changes and the action an admin needs to take.
